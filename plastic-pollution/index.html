The Great Pacific Garbage Patch

What is the Great Pacific Garbage Patch?

The Great Pacific Garbage Patch (GPGP) is a vast area of marine debris accumulation in the central North Pacific Ocean. It is composed of plastic and other waste materials that have been gathered by ocean currents, forming large 'patches' of garbage.






































Why is it a Problem?

The GPGP poses significant threats to marine life, ecosystems, and human health. Marine animals can ingest or become entangled in the debris, leading to injury or death. Additionally, the breakdown of plastics releases harmful chemicals into the water.






































What Can Be Done?

Efforts to address the GPGP include reducing plastic use, improving waste management systems, and participating in cleanup initiatives. Awareness and education are crucial to mobilizing global action to tackle this environmental issue.






































How can we help?

Well, we can start off with not polluting in the ocean, and stop using one-time plastics. We can use reuseable bags, and use metal/glass bottles now.
